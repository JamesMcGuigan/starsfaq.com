Cluster Carnage

Host: Dave Johnston
    Cluster carnage will be a game between 4 mature civilizations, each with their own
clearly defined territory, battling for possession of an equally well defined 'no mans land.'
    -Host will be playing, and game will run on autohost.
    -One turn per day, seven days a week.
    -I'm aiming for a Nov 1 start date.
    -I am looking for 3 more players, and a volunteer to set up the universe.
    -I am thinking of banning IT, as it's gating advantage would be magnified in this game, but am willing to listen to arguments either way.

Player requirements: I'm looking for advanced or expert players.  You must have won a PBEM game at higher than beginner level if you want to join, and be experienced in Nubian design/counterdesign..

The game will take place in a custom universe, shaped with 'Universe Creator' using an image that looks like this:

    Homeworlds will be adjusted as required so that each players HW (and secondary world, if any) is in its own 'corner cluster', and nobody is in the center.  Each players space consists of about 30 planets, with 8 or so in the center circle.
    In addition to their home clusters, each player will be assigned several planets in the center cluster that they are allowed to colonize or mine. (see 'Universe creation guidelines' below).  Players may negotiate trades among the center stars if they wish, but no invasions are allowed.  Planets trading hands must be evacuated and recolonized.

   In the first turn, you must:
-delete the designs of any armed or minelaying ships you start with.
-Send your scout on the shortest possible looping path that completely encloses all the worlds in both your home cluster and the center cluster.  This defines your 'area of operation'.  You must not send any ship or mineral packet outside this area for the duration of the build up period.  Note that the scout may be given orders to visit every planet in the area before making this loop; it is not important that the ship actually fly the loop, just that the path be readily visible so nobody has any doubt about where they can and cannot go.

    The game will then proceed with 10 turns of 20 years each.  During this time:
    -no armed ships or minelayers may be built.
     -No unassigned center planets may be colonized or remote mined.

    From 2600 on, turns go back to the usual one year per turn per day.  In the year 2600, minelayers may be built, but not armed ships.  From 2601 on, you may build whatever you like.

    Victory conditions:
    To win the game, a player must either:
-Own all planets in both their home cluster and the center cluster.
                        OR
-Own 40% of the universe, including ALL of the planets in their home cluster, and at least two planets in each of the other 4 clusters.

    When one of these conditions is met, the player will email his password and turn file to all other players with a declaration of victory.
    No team wins allowed.
    Victory cannot be declared before the year 2620.(20 years after regular play begins)

Universe Creation Guidelines:
    I am looking for a Stars! type person who doesn't want to play, but is willing to set up the universe.  The following are the guidelines I will ask them to follow.  The volunteer will keep generating until a universe is created that meets the following criteria.  They are open for discussion if a player has a suggestion as to how they might be improved.

    -All planets are within the white areas as shown on the bitmap.
    -Each players home (and secondary, if any) worlds are alone in their own corner cluster.
    -The largest home cluster is no more than 3 stars larger than the smallest home cluster
    -Each player will be assigned roughly 1/4 of the stars in the center cluster.  A player with fewer stars in his home cluster may be given a bonus star in the center, or a player with more stars in the home cluster may be given one less.  The player with the most center stars will have no more than 1 star more than the player with the fewest center stars.
    -It is possible that not all center stars will be assigned.
    -It is possible that not all players will have exactly the same number of planets to start.  It is understood that all Stars! games have an element of luck in the setup, and holding out for the perfect game setup could place too heavy a burden on our generous setup volunteer.
 

Sound good?

    If you are interested in playing, please email me.  If you are unknown to me, please include a brief history of your Stars! career.  Do not send race files.  Once a setup volunteer has been found, I will have you send race files and passwords directly to them.

    You will probably also like to get a feel for the universe setup, and test out potential races.  If so, you'll want to download:

    -Universe Creator (yes, I know the site is in russian.  If you scroll down in the left hand frame, however, you will find a download link in English.)
    -a copy of the actual bimap used to create the universe, and an example of the type of universe we will use; get files here

    (Actually, though the cluster sizes are identical on the bitmap, some clusters in the altered map are consistently smaller than others.  A little tweaking on the bitmap will probably be required to make an even playing field.  This shouldn't affect your testbeds, however.)

    Create a universe with the following characteristics:
    Difficulty level: Standard
    Size: Small
    Density: Normal
    Player Positions: Distant
    Accelerated BBS start
    No Random Events

    Now start the universe creator.  Set it up as follows:

    Template file: carnage.bmp
    Sensetivity: 2
    Free space mode NOT checked
    Min dist. between stars: 17
    Universe size: small
    'max search' box checked.
    xy selection: test.xy, or whatever you called the test game you just set up.

    Hit generate.  Sometimes, generation fails, or the resulting universe has stars outside the designated areas.  If so, generate again until you get a nice 'clean' map.

    Go back to your stars game and reload the turn to see the new universe layout.

Notes on moving stars:

    Universe creator allows one to move stars about on the map, subject to one major constraint.  While star positions may be changed, the left-to-right order in which the stars occur cannot change.  This is a constraint built in to the game.xy file itself.  In practice, what this means is that while a star may be shifted up or down as one desires, the left-to-right position can hardly be changed at all.  For our purposes, a star can be moved between upper and lower clusters, but not between left and right clusters.
    Also note that the map, as diplayed by Universe Creator, is a vertical reflection of the map as shown by stars.  The UC author assures me that this oversight is due to be changed in the next version.

    Once UC has generated a map, the person setting up the universe (hereafter called "God"), must verify that :
1) there are two races in the left hand clusters, and two in the right hand clusters.
2) there are approximately the same number of planets in the left two clusters as in the right two clusters.

    If both these are true, then a suitable map may be created from the initial setup.  Otherwise, God must run UC again in hopes of a more amenable setup.

    When a suitable map has been found, God must make a list of what planets need to be moved up or down, from one cluster to another, in order to isolate players homeworlds and assure a fairly even distribution of planets.  God will then go back to UC and:
-select 'change'
-in the 'change' window, click '+', to initiate a list of planets to be moved.  The UC asks for homeworlds, but in fact any planets may be moved.
-from the dropdown list, select the name of a planet to be moved.
-click on 'ins' to add the planet to the list.
-repeat these two steps until all planets to be shifted are listed.
-Click on "Next".  A blue vertical line will appear on the UC map screen, passing through the first selected planet.  Don't forget the mirror image: stars that the Stars! game show to be in the upper clusters appear in the lower clusters on the UC screen, and vice versa.  Move the cursor over the blue line, and it becomes a crosshair.  Select the spot on the line to shift the planet to, and click to move the planet to that location.  You will then be shown another vertical blue line, for the next planet to be moved.  Repeat the above procedure until all planets that need moving have been moved.
-Go back to the stars game and reload the turns.

In one sample universe I created, it took two tries to find a suitable universe (3 HW's on the left in the first try), and about 15 minutes total to set things up.  The most time consuming portion was counting the stars in the individual clusters.  After several attempts, I found the best way to be sure of counting all planets and not counting any twice was to count the clicks as each planet in a cluster was added as a waypoint for a scout.
